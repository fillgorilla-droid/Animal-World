Shark Birth & Early Life

Sharks can be oviparous (egg-laying), ovoviviparous (eggs hatch inside the mother), or viviparous (live birth), depending on the species. Baby sharks, called pups, are born fully formed and are capable of swimming and hunting immediately.

Most species give birth to 2–20 pups, though some, like tiger sharks, can have up to 80 in a single litter.

Juvenile Growth

Juvenile sharks grow rapidly in their first years, learning to hunt efficiently and avoid predators. They develop sharper teeth and stronger jaws as they mature, which helps them catch larger prey.

Some species, like great whites, may grow a few centimeters per month in their early years, reaching sexual maturity in 5–15 years depending on the species.

Adulthood & Lifespan

Sharks reach sexual maturity at different ages: smaller species at 4–6 years, large species like great whites or tiger sharks at 15–20 years. Some species can live 30 years or more, while the Greenland shark may live over 400 years, making it the longest-living vertebrate.

Adult sharks are fully capable hunters and contribute to maintaining the balance of the marine ecosystem.
