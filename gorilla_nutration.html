Gorilla Diet & Feeding Habits

Herbivorous Diet: Gorillas mainly eat leaves, stems, shoots, and fruits. Occasionally, they may consume small insects.

Daily Intake: An adult gorilla can eat 18–30 kg (40–66 lbs) of vegetation daily to meet their energy needs.

Feeding Time: Gorillas spend 8–10 hours a day foraging and eating, moving slowly through the forest to find fresh leaves and fruits.

Special Preferences: They favor fruits when available but rely on leaves and stems during dry seasons.

Despite their size and strength, gorillas are mostly gentle feeders, carefully selecting nutritious plants and rarely harming other creatures.

Gorilla Food Favorites

Leaves & Stems: The majority of a gorilla's diet is composed of young leaves, stems, and shoots. These provide fiber and essential nutrients.

Fruits: When in season, gorillas love fruits such as bananas, figs, guava, and berries. Fruit is calorie-dense and highly preferred.

Bark & Roots: In dry seasons, they eat tree bark, roots, and piths to supplement their diet.

Flowers & Seeds: Occasionally, flowers and seeds are eaten for vitamins and minerals.

Insects: Rarely, gorillas consume ants, termites, or small insects, but this is a minor part of their diet.

Overall, gorillas are selective feeders, choosing the freshest and most nutritious plant parts available in their habitat.

How Gorillas Eat & Do They Eat Meat?

Feeding Method: Gorillas eat slowly and calmly, usually sitting or crouching. They use their hands to pull leaves, break stems, and bring food to their mouth with great precision.

Use of Hands: Their strong fingers allow them to strip leaves from branches, peel fruit skins, and separate edible parts from tough fibers.

Chewing Power: Powerful jaws and large molars help them grind fibrous plants efficiently, allowing proper digestion.

Do Gorillas Eat Meat? Gorillas are primarily herbivores. They do not hunt or actively eat meat. However, they may occasionally consume small insects like ants or termites accidentally or for extra protein.

Conclusion: Gorillas rely almost entirely on plant-based food. Their strength comes from massive food intake, efficient digestion, and a highly adapted herbivorous diet — not from eating meat.
