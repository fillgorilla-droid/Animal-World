Who Is the Giant Shark?

The largest shark in the world is the whale shark (*Rhincodon typus*). It can grow up to 18–20 meters (59–65 ft) in length and weigh around 20 tons, making it the largest fish alive today.

Despite its enormous size, the whale shark is a gentle filter-feeder, eating plankton, small fish, and tiny crustaceans. It inhabits warm oceans around the world and migrates to find food-rich waters.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Up to 20 meters (65 ft).

Weight: Around 20 tons.

Mouth: Can reach 1.5 meters wide, used for filter-feeding plankton.

Fins & Tail: Large pectoral fins and crescent-shaped tail provide stability and propulsion.

The whale shark moves gracefully despite its massive size, often gliding near the ocean surface while feeding.

Feeding & Swimming Power

Whale sharks are filter-feeders, swimming with mouths wide open to filter plankton and small fish from the water. They can consume thousands of liters of water per hour.

Swimming speed is usually slow, about 5 km/h (3 mph), but they can accelerate slightly when necessary. Despite their size, they are completely harmless to humans.

Whale sharks play a crucial role in the ocean ecosystem by consuming massive amounts of plankton and helping maintain the balance of marine life.
