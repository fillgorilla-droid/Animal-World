Who Is the Giant Blue Whale?

The giant blue whale is the largest animal ever recorded on Earth. Adults can reach lengths up to 33 meters (108 ft) and weigh over 200 tons. Despite their enormous size, they are gentle filter feeders that mostly eat krill.

They live in oceans worldwide and play a crucial role in marine ecosystems, regulating krill populations and distributing nutrients.

Body Measurements

Length: Up to 33 meters (108 ft).

Weight: Over 200 tons.

Heart: Can weigh 700 kg (largest of any animal).

Tongue: Up to 4 tons, used to scoop krill.

Flippers: Up to 4.5 meters long, for steering and swimming.

Feeding Habits

Giant blue whales feed almost exclusively on krill, consuming up to 4 tons per day. They use baleen plates to filter food from seawater, taking in huge gulps and pushing the water out while trapping krill inside.

They feed intensively during summer to build fat reserves for long migrations.

Growing Up

0–1 year: Calves are 8–9 meters long, 3–4 tons, fully dependent on mother’s milk.

1–2 years: Start eating krill, reach 16–18 meters, travel with mother.

2–5 years: Juveniles grow to 22 meters, feed independently, learn migration routes.

5–10 years: Subadults 25–28 meters, sexually mature, fully independent.

10+ years: Adults 30–33 meters, over 200 tons, migrate thousands of kilometers, lifespan up to 100 years.
