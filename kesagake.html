The Kesagake Bear

The Kesagake was a massive Ussuri brown bear that became infamous in Japan in 1915. It attacked several villages in Hokkaido, becoming one of the most dangerous bears in history.

The bear was unusually large, aggressive, and fearless around humans, which made it extremely dangerous.

Size & Physical Power

Species: Ussuri brown bear (Ursus arctos lasiotus).

Estimated Weight: Around 340–380 kg (750–840 lbs).

Length: About 2.7–3 meters (9–10 ft) when standing.

Claws: Long, curved claws used for digging and attacking.

Its massive body and powerful limbs made it capable of breaking doors and walls of wooden houses.

Strength & Notoriety

Kesagake became known after a series of attacks on a rural settlement. It showed unusual persistence, returning multiple times despite being chased away.

The bear was eventually hunted down by experienced hunters after a large-scale effort.

The incident remains one of the most famous and tragic bear encounters in history.
