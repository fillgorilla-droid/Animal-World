Hamster Life from Birth to 1 Month

Birth: Hamsters are born hairless, blind, and completely dependent on their mother.

Days 1–7: They stay in the nest, feeding on mother's milk, growing rapidly.

Weeks 2–3: Fur starts to appear, eyes begin to open, and they start exploring the nest slightly.

Weeks 3–4: Hamsters begin eating solid food while still nursing, and play with siblings to develop social skills.

Hamster Life from 1 to 3 Months

1 Month: Weaning is complete, young hamsters start exploring outside the nest independently.

1–2 Months: Rapid growth continues. They learn to climb, run in wheels, and burrow.

2–3 Months: Hamsters reach sexual maturity. Social behaviors develop, though some species become territorial.

Hamster Life from 3 to 12 Months

3–6 Months: Hamsters are fully grown in size. Activity levels are high, and they explore territory actively.

6–12 Months: Social hierarchy forms if multiple hamsters are housed together. Health and diet are crucial for longevity.

Behavior: Hamsters remain nocturnal, grooming, running, storing food, and burrowing extensively.

Hamster Life from 1 to 3 Years

1–2 Years: Hamsters are considered mature adults. They maintain activity and reproduction capability, though health may slowly decline.

2–3 Years: Older hamsters show slower movement and reduced fertility. Diet, exercise, and enrichment become critical to prevent obesity and stress.

Life Expectancy: Most hamsters live 2–3 years in captivity, depending on species, care, and genetics.
