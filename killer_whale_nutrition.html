What Do Killer Whales Eat?

Killer whales are apex predators with a diverse diet. They feed on fish, squid, seals, sea lions, penguins, and even large whales. Their diet depends on the pod type and region — some pods specialize in fish, others in marine mammals.

Young orcas learn hunting strategies from their pod, gradually mastering coordinated attacks and prey selection.

How Killer Whales Eat

Killer whales hunt in groups, using teamwork to herd fish or isolate prey. They use their teeth to grab and tear apart marine mammals, while fish are usually swallowed whole.

Some pods use special techniques, like beaching briefly to catch seals, or creating waves to knock prey off ice floes.

Feeding Behavior & Hunting Strategy

Orcas are highly intelligent and social hunters. They communicate during hunts to coordinate movements. Different pods have unique hunting "techniques" passed down through generations.

By consuming large amounts of prey and hunting in pods, killer whales maintain their role as top predators and regulate marine populations effectively.
