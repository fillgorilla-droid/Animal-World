Grizzly Cubs

Grizzly bears give birth to 1–4 cubs during hibernation, usually in January or February. Cubs are born blind, tiny (about 0.5 kg), and completely dependent on their mother.

For the first few months, cubs stay in the den, nursing and growing rapidly. By spring, they begin exploring outside under their mother’s watchful eye.

Juvenile Bears

After leaving the den, cubs learn essential survival skills such as foraging, fishing, and climbing. They stay with their mother for 2–3 years, learning hunting techniques and navigating their territory.

During this time, they grow rapidly, gaining strength and coordination to become independent.

Adult Grizzlies

Grizzly bears reach full size at 5–7 years old, with males larger than females. Adults are solitary, except during mating season or at abundant food sources like salmon streams.

Adult bears establish territories and continue to refine hunting and foraging skills. They can live up to 25 years in the wild.
