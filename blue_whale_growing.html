0–1 Year: Calf Stage

Blue whale calves are born about 7–8 meters (23–26 ft) long and weigh 2–3 tons. During the first year, they are fully dependent on their mother’s milk, which is very rich in fat to help them grow quickly. By the end of the year, they can reach 10–12 meters in length.

1–2 Years: Young Calf

At 1–2 years old, the calf starts to try eating krill, gradually weaning off milk. Growth continues rapidly — the whale can reach 15–16 meters. They travel with their mother, learning feeding grounds and navigation.

2–5 Years: Juvenile Stage

From 2 to 5 years old, juveniles grow fast, reaching up to 20 meters. They continue learning to feed independently and discover migration routes. Their diet becomes more varied, eating mostly krill and occasionally small fish.

5–10 Years: Subadult Stage

Between 5 and 10 years, subadult whales continue growing, reaching 24–27 meters. Sexual maturity occurs around 8–10 years. They become more independent, actively migrating and finding large krill concentrations.

10+ Years: Adult Stage

Adult blue whales reach 27–30 meters in length and can weigh over 180 tons. They are fully independent, able to migrate thousands of kilometers and store fat for long journeys. Lifespan can reach 80–90 years in the wild.
