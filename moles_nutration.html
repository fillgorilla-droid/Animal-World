Mole Diet & Feeding Habits

Moles are insectivores, which means they mainly eat insects and small creatures found in the soil. Their favorite food is earthworms.

They hunt by moving through their tunnels and sensing vibrations with their sensitive snouts.

Moles have a very fast metabolism and must eat frequently to stay alive.

Favorite Foods

Main Food: Earthworms.

Other Prey: Beetle larvae, ants, slugs, centipedes, and small insects.

Water Source: Moisture from worms and other prey.

Moles sometimes store earthworms in special underground chambers so they can eat them later.

Daily Feeding

Daily Intake: Up to its own body weight in food.

Activity: Hunts both day and night.

Storage: Keeps paralyzed worms in tunnel chambers.

Because of their high energy needs, moles cannot survive long without food and must hunt constantly.
