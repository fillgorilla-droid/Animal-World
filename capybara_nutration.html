Capybara Diet & Feeding Habits

Capybaras are strict herbivores. Their diet mainly consists of grasses, aquatic plants, and soft vegetation found near rivers and wetlands.

They spend many hours each day grazing, especially in the early morning and late afternoon when temperatures are cooler.

During dry seasons, capybaras may eat bark, reeds, and tougher plants when fresh grass is harder to find.

Favorite Foods of Capybaras

Grasses: The main part of their diet. Capybaras graze like cows and can eat large amounts daily.

Aquatic Plants: Water plants and reeds are important food sources near rivers and lakes.

Fruits: Occasionally, they eat fallen fruits when available.

Bark & Leaves: In dry periods, they chew on bark, leaves, and tougher vegetation.

Capybaras are selective feeders and prefer fresh, tender plants.

How Capybaras Eat

Feeding Method: Capybaras graze slowly, using their sharp front teeth to cut grass and plants.

Ever-Growing Teeth: Their incisors grow constantly, so chewing tough plants helps keep them at the right length.

Coprophagy: Capybaras sometimes eat their own soft droppings. This helps them digest nutrients and vitamins that were not absorbed the first time.

Daily Routine: They usually feed during cooler parts of the day and rest in the water during hot hours.

These feeding habits help capybaras survive in wetland environments with changing food availability.
