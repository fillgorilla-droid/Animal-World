What Do Blue Whales Eat?

Blue whales feed almost exclusively on tiny shrimp-like animals called krill. Despite their enormous size, they consume very small prey in massive quantities.

An adult blue whale can eat up to 4 tons of krill per day during feeding season. They are filter feeders and swallow large amounts of water along with their prey.

How Blue Whales Eat

Blue whales use their huge mouths and baleen plates to filter krill from seawater. They take a deep gulp, closing their mouths and pushing the water out through the baleen, trapping krill inside.

This method allows them to eat enormous amounts of food in a single feeding session. They often feed in areas with high krill concentrations, such as cold ocean currents.

Feeding Behavior & Seasonal Diet

Blue whales feed intensively in summer when krill is abundant, gaining fat reserves for the winter migration.

During migration, they may feed less or not at all, relying on stored energy. Their feeding behavior is crucial for maintaining their massive size and energy requirements.

By consuming such large amounts of krill, blue whales also play an important role in the ocean ecosystem, controlling krill populations and distributing nutrients through their waste.
