0–1 Year: Calf Stage

Sperm whale calves are born about 3–4 meters (10–13 ft) long and weigh around 1 ton. During the first year, they are fully dependent on their mother's milk, which is extremely rich in fat to help them grow quickly. By the end of the year, they can reach 5–6 meters in length.

1–3 Years: Young Calf

At 1–3 years old, the calf starts to try catching small fish and squid, gradually weaning off milk. Growth continues rapidly — the whale can reach 8–10 meters. They travel with their mother and learn hunting and diving techniques.

3–8 Years: Juvenile Stage

From 3 to 8 years old, juveniles grow fast, reaching 12–15 meters. They continue learning to dive deeply and hunt independently. Their diet becomes more focused on squid, and they start accompanying other whales on deep-sea hunts.

8–15 Years: Subadult Stage

Subadult whales continue growing, reaching 16–18 meters. Sexual maturity begins around 10–12 years. They become more independent, participate in hunting expeditions, and explore larger territories.

15+ Years: Adult Stage

Adult sperm whales reach 16–20 meters for females and up to 20.5 meters for males, weighing up to 57 tons. They are fully independent, capable of deep-sea hunting and long migrations. Lifespan can reach 70–80 years in the wild.
