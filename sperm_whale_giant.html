Who Are Giant Sperm Whales?

Giant sperm whales are the largest toothed predators in the ocean. Adult males can reach lengths of up to 20.5 meters (67 ft) and weigh up to 57 tons. They are deep-diving specialists, hunting squid, fish, and other deep-sea creatures.

Sperm whales live in social groups of females and calves, while adult males are mostly solitary. They communicate using clicks and codas, essential for hunting coordination and social bonds.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Males up to 20.5 meters (67 ft), females up to 12 meters (39 ft).

Weight: Males up to 57 tons, females ~14 tons.

Head: Massive rectangular head with spermaceti organ for echolocation and buoyancy.

Teeth: Large conical teeth on lower jaw for catching squid.

Flippers & Tail: Flippers up to 2.5 m, tail fluke 5–6 m for powerful swimming.

Diving & Hunting Power

Giant sperm whales are the deepest diving mammals, capable of diving over 2,000 meters (6,500 ft) and holding their breath for 90+ minutes.

Their massive tail and strong muscles allow high propulsion to chase deep-sea prey.

Despite their strength, they are careful predators, mainly feeding on squid and fish, and rarely aggressive to other whales.

What Do Giant Sperm Whales Eat?

Giant sperm whales are carnivorous, feeding mostly on giant squid, octopuses, and deep-sea fish. They can swallow prey whole thanks to their strong jaws and conical teeth.

They use echolocation clicks from the spermaceti organ to locate prey in deep darkness. Adult males can consume dozens of squid per dive.
