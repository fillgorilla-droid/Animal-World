The Largest Bear in the World

The polar bear is the largest bear species and the biggest land carnivore on Earth. These massive predators live in the Arctic and are perfectly adapted to icy environments.

Adult males, called boars, usually weigh between 350–700 kg (770–1,540 lbs), but the largest recorded polar bear weighed over 1,000 kg (2,200 lbs).

Size & Physical Power

Length: Up to 3 meters (10 ft) when standing on hind legs.

Weight: Typically 350–700 kg; record over 1,000 kg.

Paws: Up to 30 cm wide, used like snowshoes on ice.

Fur & Fat: Thick white fur and a fat layer up to 11 cm thick for insulation.

Their powerful limbs and massive bodies allow them to swim long distances and hunt large prey.

Strength & Hunting Ability

Polar bears are apex predators, mainly hunting seals. They can smell prey from over a kilometer away and break through ice with powerful forelimbs.

Swimming Speed: About 10 km/h (6 mph).

Running Speed: Up to 40 km/h (25 mph) in short bursts.

Despite their enormous size, they are excellent swimmers and can travel hundreds of kilometers across icy waters.
