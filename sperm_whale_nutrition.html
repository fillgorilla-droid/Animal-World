What Do Sperm Whales Eat?

Sperm whales are carnivorous and mainly feed on deep-sea creatures like giant squid, octopuses, and various fish. Their diet is adapted to their deep-diving lifestyle, hunting prey at depths of 1,000–2,000 meters.

Adult males consume enormous amounts of squid, often swallowing them whole using their large jaws and conical teeth.

How Sperm Whales Eat

Sperm whales locate prey using echolocation clicks produced by their spermaceti organ. They dive silently, detect movements, and attack with strong jaws and teeth.

Once the prey is caught, they swallow it whole. They can consume dozens of squid in a single deep dive session.

Feeding Behavior & Deep-Sea Hunting

Sperm whales feed mostly at night, when squid migrate closer to the surface. They dive in groups or alone, sometimes coordinating to locate food-rich areas.

By feeding on giant squid and other deep-sea animals, sperm whales maintain a key role in the ocean ecosystem, controlling populations and transferring nutrients from deep waters to the surface.
