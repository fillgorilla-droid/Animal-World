The Largest Capybara Ever Recorded

The largest capybaras are rare individuals that grow far beyond the typical size of the species.

The biggest recorded capybaras reached weights of 90–100 kilograms (200–220 lbs), much heavier than the average 35–65 kg capybara.

These giant capybaras are usually males and are extremely rare in the wild.

Physical Strength

While capybaras are not predators, the largest individuals have impressive physical power for their size.

Strong legs allow them to swim quickly, escape predators, and move efficiently on land.

Their large size gives them better survival chances in the wild and dominance within their social groups.

Habitat of Giant Capybaras

The largest capybaras are found in lush wetlands and river systems of South America.

Countries such as Brazil, Venezuela, and Argentina have some of the largest recorded individuals.

Abundant grasses and aquatic plants in these habitats allow them to grow larger than capybaras living in less rich areas.

Diet of the Largest Capybaras

Giant capybaras need a nutrient-rich diet to maintain their size and weight.

They primarily feed on grasses, aquatic plants, reeds, and occasionally fruits.

Feeding many hours per day ensures they get enough energy to sustain their massive bodies.

Their unique habit of coprophagy (eating soft droppings) helps them absorb additional nutrients efficiently.

Social Status of Giant Capybaras

Large capybaras are often dominant within their groups due to their size.

Dominance provides priority access to the best feeding areas and mates.

Despite their size, even the largest capybaras remain gentle and social, maintaining bonds with the group.
