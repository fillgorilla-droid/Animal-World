Largest Giant Squid Ever Recorded

The largest giant squid (Architeuthis dux) ever documented reached an astonishing length of over 13 meters (43 feet) for females, with males slightly smaller, around 10 meters (33 feet).

These colossal creatures inhabit deep ocean waters, typically between 300 and 1000 meters, and are rarely seen by humans.

Their massive size allows them to capture large prey, including fish and smaller squid, and makes them one of the ocean's most formidable invertebrate predators.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Up to 13 meters (43 ft) for females, 10 meters (33 ft) for males.

Weight: Around 275–500 kg (600–1100 lbs), depending on age and condition.

Tentacles: Long, powerful tentacles with suction cups for grasping prey.

Beak: Sharp, strong beak used for cutting and consuming prey.

Eyes: Largest in the animal kingdom, up to 27 cm (11 inches) across, adapted for deep-sea vision.

Hunting & Strength

Giant squids are deep-sea predators, hunting mainly at night using their long tentacles to capture prey like fish and smaller squids.

Their large eyes allow them to detect bioluminescent light in the darkness of the deep ocean, while powerful muscles and suction cups secure prey for consumption.

Despite their size, giant squids are elusive, fast swimmers and can evade predators such as sperm whales, which are their main natural threat.
