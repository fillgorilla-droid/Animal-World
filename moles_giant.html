Meet the Giant Mole

The Giant Mole is the largest species of mole in the world. It can reach up to 50 cm in length and weigh more than 1.5 kg.

Its body is specially adapted for digging through tough soil and hunting larger prey underground.

Body Features

Length: Up to 50 cm.

Weight: Around 1.5 kg or more.

Fur: Thick and dense to protect from rough soil.

Paws: Extra-large, strong, perfect for digging deep tunnels.

Teeth: Sharp and strong, can catch bigger insects and worms.

Habits & Life

Giant moles live mostly alone and dig extensive tunnel networks.

They can survive in tougher environments than smaller moles, including rocky and compact soils.

Despite their size, they are rarely seen because they spend almost all their life underground.

Their lifespan is usually 3–5 years in the wild.
