Who Are Giant Killer Whales?

Giant killer whales are the largest individuals of the orca species. Adult males can exceed 10 meters (33 ft) in length and weigh more than 12 tons, making them the most massive members of the dolphin family.

Despite their size, they remain apex predators, hunting large whales, seals, and schools of fish using intelligence and coordinated strategies.

These giants have long dorsal fins up to 2 meters high, massive tails, and powerful jaws with up to 56 teeth.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Males up to 10 meters (33 ft), females up to 8 meters (26 ft).

Weight: Males up to 12 tons, females around 8 tons.

Dorsal Fin: Males’ fins reach up to 2 meters tall; females shorter but still imposing.

Teeth: 40–56 large conical teeth, perfect for capturing big prey.

Tail & Flippers: Massive tail flukes provide incredible thrust; rounded pectoral flippers allow agile movement despite size.

Hunting & Power

Giant killer whales are top predators with exceptional intelligence and social coordination. They hunt large whales, seals, and fish in pods, using complex strategies to isolate and capture prey.

They can swim up to 55 km/h (34 mph) in short bursts to catch fast-moving animals. Their size and strength allow them to dominate nearly any prey in their habitat.

Despite being apex predators, they maintain strong family and pod structures, teaching younger whales hunting techniques and survival skills.
