What Do Dolphins Eat?

Dolphins are carnivorous mammals that feed mostly on fish and squid. Their diet depends on the species and the region they inhabit. Coastal dolphins often eat small schooling fish, while deep-water dolphins may hunt squid and larger fish.

They are opportunistic feeders and can adapt to the available prey, sometimes consuming shrimp or crustaceans.

How Dolphins Hunt

Dolphins use echolocation to detect fish and squid, emitting clicks and listening for echoes to locate prey with incredible precision. They often hunt in groups, herding fish into tight balls to make feeding more efficient.

Dolphins catch prey with their conical teeth and swallow it whole. Young dolphins learn hunting techniques from their mothers and pod members.

Feeding Behavior & Strategy

Dolphins are social hunters, coordinating movements and using teamwork to maximize success. Some species, like the bottlenose dolphin, use tools such as sponges to protect their snouts while foraging on the sea floor.

By controlling fish and squid populations, dolphins play an important role in maintaining a balanced marine ecosystem.
