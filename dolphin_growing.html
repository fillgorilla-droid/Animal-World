0–1 Year: Calf Stage

Dolphin calves are born about 0.8–1.2 meters (2.5–4 ft) long and weigh around 10–15 kg (22–33 lbs). They rely entirely on their mother’s milk for the first few months, which is rich in fat and nutrients to support rapid growth.

During this stage, calves learn to swim alongside their mothers and other pod members, practicing breathing and echolocation.

1–3 Years: Juvenile Stage

Juvenile dolphins start experimenting with solid food, like fish and squid, while still nursing occasionally. Growth continues steadily, and they develop stronger swimming and hunting skills under the guidance of adults.

Social skills are also refined during this stage, as juveniles play with pod members and learn coordinated hunting strategies.

3–7 Years: Subadult Stage

Subadult dolphins grow close to adult size and become fully capable hunters. They may leave the immediate vicinity of their mothers but remain within the pod for social learning.

Physical development completes around this time, with streamlined bodies, strong flippers, and fully developed teeth.

7+ Years: Adult Stage

Adult dolphins reach their full size, with males typically 2.5–4 meters (8–13 ft) long and females slightly smaller. They are fully independent, capable of long migrations, complex hunting, and social leadership within the pod.

Lifespan varies by species, but dolphins can live 30–60 years in the wild, maintaining strong social bonds throughout their lives.
