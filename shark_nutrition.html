What Do Sharks Eat?

Sharks are carnivorous and their diet depends on the species. Most feed on fish, squid, seals, or smaller sharks. Apex predators like great whites and tiger sharks hunt larger prey, while whale sharks and basking sharks are filter feeders consuming plankton and small fish.

How Sharks Hunt

Sharks use a combination of speed, stealth, and highly developed senses to detect prey. They can sense electrical fields produced by animals, smell blood from miles away, and track movements with their lateral line system.

Predatory sharks often ambush from below, striking with powerful jaws and sharp teeth. Filter-feeding sharks swim with mouths open to capture plankton.

Feeding Behavior & Ecological Role

Sharks are important for ocean health. By feeding on weak, sick, or overpopulated species, they maintain balance in the ecosystem. Some species hunt alone, while others may hunt in groups to corral prey.

Filter-feeding species, like whale sharks, help recycle nutrients by consuming plankton and small organisms, transferring energy through the food chain.
