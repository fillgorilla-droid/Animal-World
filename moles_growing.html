Birth to 1 Month

Baby moles are born blind, hairless, and very small. They stay in the nest with their mother and depend completely on her for warmth and milk.

After a few weeks, their fur starts to grow, and their eyes slowly open.

1–3 Months

Young moles begin to leave the nest and explore the tunnel system.

They start learning how to dig and hunt for worms and insects on their own.

At the end of this stage, they become independent and leave their mother’s territory.

6–12 Months

At this age, the mole is fully grown and lives alone. It builds its own tunnel system and hunts daily.

The mole becomes strong and experienced at digging and finding food underground.

1–3 Years

This is the adult stage of a mole’s life. It controls its own territory and mates during the breeding season.

Most moles live about 2–4 years in the wild, depending on food supply and predators.
