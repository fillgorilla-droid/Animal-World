What Do Grizzly Bears Eat?

Grizzly bears are omnivores with a very varied diet. They eat berries, nuts, roots, and plants, but also hunt fish, small mammals, and sometimes large prey like deer or moose.

Their diet depends on the season: in spring they eat more vegetation and carrion, in summer they focus on berries and insects, and in fall they consume large amounts of salmon to prepare for hibernation.

Hunting & Foraging Techniques

Grizzly bears have a strong sense of smell and can locate food from miles away. They dig for roots and small animals, catch fish in rivers and streams, and use their strength to hunt larger prey when needed.

Bears are opportunistic feeders, meaning they will eat whatever is available in their habitat, making them highly adaptable.
