Who Are Blue Whales?

Blue whales are the largest animals ever known to have lived on Earth. Adults can reach lengths of up to 30 meters (98 feet) and weigh over 180 tons.

Despite their enormous size, blue whales are gentle giants and feed mainly on small shrimp-like animals called krill. They are marine mammals that live in oceans all around the world.

Blue whales communicate using low-frequency sounds that can travel hundreds of miles underwater. They play a crucial role in marine ecosystems by regulating krill populations and supporting the ocean food chain.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Up to 30 meters (98 ft).

Weight: Over 180 tons for adults.

Heart: Weighs about 600 kg, largest of any animal.

Tongue: Can weigh up to 3 tons, used to scoop krill.

Flippers: Up to 4 meters long, help with steering and swimming.

Blue whales are perfectly adapted to life in the open ocean, moving gracefully despite their enormous size.

Bite & Swimming Power

Bite: Blue whales do not bite large prey; they filter-feed using baleen plates to trap krill and plankton.

Swimming Speed: Can reach 20 mph (32 km/h) in short bursts, usually cruise at 5 mph (8 km/h).

Blowhole: Exhales large spouts of air up to 9 meters high.

Strength: Muscular tail provides incredible propulsion to move such a massive body.

Despite their size, blue whales are peaceful, non-aggressive, and feed gently on tiny marine creatures.
