Who Are Sperm Whales?

Sperm whales are the largest toothed whales and one of the most powerful predators in the ocean. Adult males can reach lengths of up to 20.5 meters (67 ft) and weigh around 57 tons.

They are deep divers, hunting giant squid and other deep-sea creatures, and can dive over 2,000 meters (6,500 ft) for more than an hour. Sperm whales live in social groups, mainly females with calves, while adult males are more solitary.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Males up to 20.5 meters (67 ft), females up to 12 meters (39 ft).

Weight: Males up to 57 tons, females around 14 tons.

Head: Massive, rectangular, containing spermaceti organ used for echolocation and buoyancy.

Teeth: Large conical teeth on the lower jaw, used to catch squid.

Flippers & Tail: Flippers up to 2.5 meters, tail fluke spans 5–6 meters for powerful swimming.

Diving & Hunting Power

Sperm whales are the deepest diving mammals, capable of diving over 2,000 meters to hunt giant squid. They hold their breath for more than 90 minutes while hunting.

Their large heads contain the spermaceti organ, which aids in echolocation and buoyancy control during deep dives.

Sperm whales feed mainly on squid, fish, and octopuses, swallowing them whole using strong jaws and teeth.

Despite their immense power and size, sperm whales are social and communicate using clicks and codas for coordination.
