Who Is the Giant Dolphin?

The largest dolphin species is the **orca (killer whale)**, which, despite being called a "dolphin," is the biggest member of the dolphin family. Adult males can reach up to 9–10 meters (30–33 ft) in length and weigh over 10 tons, while females are slightly smaller.

Giant dolphins are apex predators, living in social pods and hunting a wide range of prey from fish to large whales. They are highly intelligent, with strong family bonds and sophisticated communication.

Body Measurements & Size

Length: Males up to 10 meters (33 ft), females up to 8 meters (26 ft).

Weight: Males up to 10 tons, females up to 7 tons.

Dorsal Fin: Tall, triangular, up to 2 meters high in males.

Teeth: 40–56 large conical teeth used to catch and hold prey.

Flippers: Rounded, strong for agile swimming and maneuvering.

Hunting & Strength

Giant dolphins are apex predators, hunting in coordinated pods using intelligence and teamwork. They can take down large prey including seals, sea lions, and even large whales.

They reach swimming speeds up to 34 mph (55 km/h) in short bursts, making them powerful and agile hunters.

Despite their enormous size and hunting abilities, they maintain complex social structures and communicate using clicks, whistles, and pulsed calls.
