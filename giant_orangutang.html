Giant Orangutan

The Largest Orangutan Ever Recorded

Species: Bornean Orangutan (Adult Male)

Estimated Weight: Up to 120–140 kg (265–308 lbs)

Estimated Height: About 1.4–1.5 m (4.6–5 ft) standing

Arm Span: Up to 2.2–2.4 meters (7–8 ft)

The largest recorded orangutans are massive adult males from Borneo. These individuals grow much bigger than average males, especially when they develop full cheek pads and throat sacs.

Their size depends on genetics, diet, and territory. Well-fed dominant males can become extremely large and powerful.

Strength & Physical Power

Arm Strength: Extremely powerful for climbing and hanging

Grip Strength: Strong enough to support full body weight with one arm

Pulling Power: Capable of breaking thick branches and bending young trees

Orangutans are not built like gorillas, but their upper body strength is incredible. Their long arms allow them to swing and move through the forest canopy with ease.

Most of their strength is concentrated in the shoulders, arms, and hands, which are perfectly adapted for life in trees.

Diet Behind the Size

Daily Food Intake: Up to 10–15 kg of fruits and vegetation

Main Foods: Fruits, leaves, bark, shoots, flowers, and seeds

Large orangutans grow their size mainly from high-energy fruits. Fruits provide the calories needed to support their massive bodies.

During seasons with less fruit, they rely on leaves, bark, and other plant materials to survive.

Their slow metabolism and efficient digestion help them maintain strength on a mostly plant-based diet.

Behavior & Presence

The largest male orangutans are solitary and highly territorial. Their deep, long calls can be heard over long distances through the forest.

These calls warn other males to stay away and attract females. Fully grown males rarely fight, as their size alone is often enough to establish dominance.

Despite their strength, orangutans are generally calm and intelligent animals, spending most of their time quietly moving through the treetops.

Their presence in the forest is more silent and mysterious compared to the loud dominance of silverback gorillas.
